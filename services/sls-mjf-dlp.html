SLS / MJF / DLP 3D Printing

Advanced Industrial 3D Printing Technologies

What is SLS?

SLS (Selective Laser Sintering) is a 3D printing technology that uses a high-power laser to fuse powdered material (usually Nylon/PA12) layer by layer to create strong and functional parts. Unlike FDM, SLS does not require support structures because the surrounding powder supports the part.

🔧 How SLS Works:

  • A thin layer of powder is spread on the build platform
  • Laser selectively fuses the powder as per CAD design
  • Platform lowers and new powder layer is spread
  • Process repeats layer by layer
  • Excess powder is removed after printing

⭐ Advantages of SLS

No support required
Strong & durable parts
Complex geometries possible
Good for batch production

What is MJF?

MJF (Multi Jet Fusion) is a powder-based 3D printing technology developed by HP. It uses a special fusing agent and detailing agent sprayed onto nylon powder, then heat is applied to fuse the material layer by layer.

🔧 How MJF Works:

  • A thin layer of nylon powder (PA12/PA11) is spread
  • Inkjet heads spray fusing & detailing agents
  • Infrared heat melts selected areas
  • Platform lowers and new powder layer is spread
  • Unfused powder is removed after printing

⭐ Advantages of MJF

High strength & durability
Excellent surface finish
No support structures needed
Fast production speed

What is DLP?

DLP (Digital Light Processing) is a resin-based 3D printing technology that uses a digital projector screen to cure liquid photopolymer resin layer by layer. Unlike SLA (which uses a laser), DLP cures an entire layer at once, making it faster.

🔧 How DLP Works:

  • Liquid resin is filled in a vat
  • Digital projector flashes an entire layer image
  • Resin hardens instantly
  • Platform moves up layer by layer
  • Final part is cleaned & UV cured

⭐ Advantages of DLP

Very high accuracy
Smooth surface finish
Faster than SLA
Ideal for small & detailed parts
